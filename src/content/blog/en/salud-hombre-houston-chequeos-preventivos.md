# Men's Health in Houston: Preventive Checkups That Can Save Your Life

Many men only see a doctor when something hurts. The problem is that the conditions that most affect men's health — high blood pressure, diabetes, high cholesterol, prostate problems — progress for years without any symptoms. At Clínica Hispana Cruz #3 we see Hispanic men from all over Houston every day who caught a condition early thanks to a simple checkup.

## Why Should Men Get Regular Checkups?

High blood pressure doesn't hurt. Early-stage diabetes can't be felt. High cholesterol gives no warning until it has already affected your heart or arteries. That's why they're called "silent diseases."

An annual checkup allows you to:

- Detect hypertension, diabetes and high cholesterol before they cause damage
- Monitor prostate health starting at the recommended age
- Check your testosterone levels if you notice fatigue or mood changes
- Prevent serious complications like heart attacks and strokes
- Have a year-over-year baseline for comparison

## Our Men's Health Services

### General Men's Checkup
A complete evaluation including blood pressure, weight, vital signs, physical review and a medical consultation in Spanish to discuss any concern.

### Prostate Exam (PSA)
The prostate-specific antigen (PSA) is a blood test that helps monitor prostate health and detect changes early. Our [men's health services](/services/salud-hombre) include this test with clearly explained results.

### Male Hormone Panel
Testosterone affects your energy, mood, muscle mass and intimate life. If you feel tired for no reason or have noticed changes, a testosterone measurement can provide answers.

### Lab Work
We offer [blood tests](/services/examenes-sangre) in our own on-site laboratory: glucose, cholesterol, triglycerides, kidney and liver function, and more — no need to go anywhere else.

### Electrocardiogram
An [electrocardiogram](/services/electrocardiograma) evaluates your heart's rhythm and electrical activity. It's quick, painless and provides valuable information, especially after age 40.

## Recommended Exams by Age

| Age | Recommended Exams |
|------|----------------------|
| 18-29 | Blood pressure, general checkup, STD testing if sexually active |
| 30-39 | The above + glucose, cholesterol and annual weight check |
| 40-49 | The above + PSA if family history, electrocardiogram |
| 50+ | The above + annual PSA and colon health evaluation |

These are general guidelines. Your doctor may recommend additional exams based on your personal and family history.

## Warning Signs: Don't Ignore Them

See a doctor as soon as possible if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure
- Excessive thirst, frequent urination or unexplained weight loss
- Difficulty, burning or blood when urinating
- Persistent fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Frequent headaches
- Changes in sexual performance
- Lumps or changes in the testicles

## Practical Tips for Taking Care of Your Health

1. **Get an annual checkup** even if you feel fine — that's the whole point of prevention
2. **Know your numbers:** blood pressure, glucose and cholesterol
3. **Move 30 minutes a day:** walking counts
4. **Cut back on sodas and fried food** — small changes add up
5. **Sleep 7 hours** on a regular schedule
6. **Don't wait for pain:** pain usually arrives when the condition is already advanced
